# Course Description

This course introduces kindergarten students to foundational reading skills.
It is designed to focus on creating phonemic awareness and letter sound correspondence.

## Target Audience

Kindergarten students (ages 5-6)

## Prerequisites

None - this course is designed for beginning readers.

## List of Topics

- Sounds of English
- Letters of English
- One-to-one Correspondence
- Sound-Letter Correspondence
- Blending sounds into words

## Topics Excluded

- Parts of Speech
- Sentences
- Verb tenses
- Reading beyond the word level
- Irregular sight words
- Spelling
- Digraphs
- Blends
- Vowel Teams
- Silent E

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Develop the concept of one-to-one correspondence
1. Count the number of spoken words in a short sentence
3. Recognize the first sound in a spoken word
1. Recognize letters of the alphabet
2. Understand basic phonics
3. Associate consonant letters with their most common sound
1. Associate five short vowel sounds and the six long vowel sounds with the five letters representing vowels
1. Blend vowel-consonant words and consonant-vowel-consonant words using the vowel sound letter sounds taught in the prior step
1. Recognize 18 sight words

## Bloom's Taxonomy Learning Objectives

The learning outcomes above are categorized by Bloom's Taxonomy cognitive levels:

### Remember (Knowledge)
- Recognize all 26 letters of the alphabet (uppercase and lowercase)
- Recall the most common sound for each consonant letter
- Identify the five short vowel sounds and six long vowel sounds
- Recognize 18 high-frequency sight words

### Understand (Comprehension)
- Demonstrate one-to-one correspondence between spoken and written words
- Count the number of spoken words in a short sentence
- Explain that letters represent sounds in spoken words
- Understand that words are made up of individual sounds (phonemes)

### Apply (Application)
- Associate consonant letters with their most common sounds
- Match vowel letters to their short and long sounds
- Blend vowel-consonant (VC) words using learned letter sounds
- Blend consonant-vowel-consonant (CVC) words using learned letter sounds

### Analyze (Analysis)
- Identify the first sound in a spoken word
- Segment words into individual sounds
- Distinguish between different vowel sounds in words

### Create
- Create their own small words including nonsense words
- Use gestures and arm motions to associate motion with the shape of a letter

## Evaluation

- Accuracy of sound letter correspondence to sounds taught
- Accuracy when blending sounds into words
- Mastery of specific learning objectives

Note: we do not do percentage of scores at this young age level
