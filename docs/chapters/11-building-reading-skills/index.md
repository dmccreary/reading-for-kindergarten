# Chapter 11: Building Reading Skills

## Overview

This capstone chapter brings together all previously learned skills to develop independent reading abilities. Children will apply their knowledge of letters, sounds, and words to read simple texts on their own.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:

- **Remember**: Apply letter-sound knowledge automatically
- **Understand**: Comprehend simple texts
- **Apply**: Read emergent reader books independently
- **Create**: Build and write simple words and sentences

## Chapter Summary

This final chapter celebrates how far young readers have come! Children will apply all their skillsletter recognition, phonemic awareness, phonics, sight words, and fluencyto read simple books independently. This is where all the pieces come together for the joy of reading!

## Key Vocabulary

- **Independent**: Doing it by yourself
- **Comprehension**: Understanding what you read
- **Emergent reader**: Someone just learning to read
- **Retell**: Tell the story again in your own words

## Activities

1. **Independent Reading Time**: Read self-selected books quietly
2. **Story Retelling**: Share what happened in a book
3. **Word Building Games**: Create words using letter tiles
4. **Reading Celebration**: Share favorite books with the class
5. **Reading Goals**: Set personal reading goals
