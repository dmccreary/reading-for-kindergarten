    2. This is expected as it includes all 52 letters (26 uppercase + 26 lowercase)

    3. Does not exceed the 30% threshold

    4. Well-Balanced Categories

    6. No single category dominates the learning graph

    7. Appropriate Capstone Size

    10. Foundation-Heavy Structure

    12. Appropriate for kindergarten-level reading instruction

    Category Details¶

    FOUND - Foundation Concepts (15 concepts)¶

    Core prerequisites including spoken language, listening, print awareness, and letter concepts.

    LETR - Letter Recognition (52 concepts)¶

    All uppercase (A-Z) and lowercase (a-z) letters.

    PHON - Phonemic Awareness (13 concepts)¶

    Sound awareness skills including initial/final/medial sounds, rhyming, and syllables.

    CONS - Consonant Sounds (23 concepts)¶

    21 consonant letter sounds plus the general consonant concept.

    VOWL - Vowel Sounds (14 concepts)¶

    5 short vowel sounds, 5 long vowel sounds, plus general vowel concepts.

    LINK - Letter-Sound Links (8 concepts)¶

    Connecting letters to sounds, including formation and kinesthetic learning.

    BLND - Blending Skills (15 concepts)¶

    Sound blending, word patterns (VC, CVC), and decoding.

    SGHT - Sight Words (20 concepts)¶

    18 specific high-frequency sight words plus general sight word concepts.

    ASMT - Practice & Assessment (15 concepts)¶

    Repetition, practice, mastery, fluency, and assessment concepts.

    APPL - Application Skills (10 concepts)¶

    Word creation, manipulation, and pattern recognition.

    CAPS - Capstone Skills (10 concepts)¶

    Independent reading and emergent reader skills.

    Recommendations¶

    1. No Over-Representation Issues
    2. All categories are within acceptable ranges

    3. No category exceeds the 30% threshold

    4. Consider Future Expansion

    5. LINK category is smallest (4.1%) - could expand with more kinesthetic/multisensory concepts

    6. APPL could include more word manipulation exercises

    7. Learning Path Balance

    8. Good progression from foundation → skills → application → capstone
    9. Categories support multiple learning pathways
