    Concept Taxonomy for Reading for Kindergarten¶

    This document defines the categorical taxonomy for organizing concepts in the learning graph.

    Taxonomy Categories¶

    Category Name TaxonomyID Description
    Foundation Concepts FOUND Core prerequisite concepts including spoken language, listening, and print awareness
    Letter Recognition LETR Individual letter concepts (uppercase and lowercase)
    Phonemic Awareness PHON Skills related to hearing and manipulating sounds in words
    Consonant Sounds CONS Individual consonant sounds and letter-sound correspondence
    Vowel Sounds VOWL Short and long vowel sounds
    Letter-Sound Links LINK Connecting letters to their sounds, including formation and kinesthetic learning
    Blending Skills BLND Sound blending, word patterns, and decoding skills
    Sight Words SGHT High-frequency sight words
    Practice & Assessment ASMT Repetition, practice, mastery, and assessment concepts
    Application Skills APPL Word creation, manipulation, and pattern recognition
    Capstone Skills CAPS Independent reading and emergent reader skills

    Category Descriptions¶

    FOUND - Foundation Concepts¶

    Concepts that form the basic prerequisites for reading instruction. These include oral language skills, listening abilities, and awareness of print and books.

    ConceptIDs: 1-15

    LETR - Letter Recognition¶

    Individual letter concepts covering both uppercase (A-Z) and lowercase (a-z) letter recognition.

    ConceptIDs: 16-67

    PHON - Phonemic Awareness¶

    Skills for identifying, isolating, and manipulating individual sounds (phonemes) in spoken words. Includes rhyming, syllable awareness, and sound segmentation.

    ConceptIDs: 68-80

    CONS - Consonant Sounds¶

    The sounds associated with consonant letters (B, C, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N, P, Q, R, S, T, V, W, X, Y, Z).

    ConceptIDs: 81-103

    VOWL - Vowel Sounds¶

    Short and long vowel sounds for the five vowel letters (A, E, I, O, U).

    ConceptIDs: 104-117

    LINK - Letter-Sound Links¶

    Concepts connecting visual letters to their sounds, including letter formation, tracing, and kinesthetic approaches.

    ConceptIDs: 118-125

    BLND - Blending Skills¶

    The process of combining individual sounds to form words. Includes VC and CVC word patterns, oral blending, and decoding.

    ConceptIDs: 126-140

    SGHT - Sight Words¶

    High-frequency words that students learn to recognize by sight (the, a, is, it, in, to, and, I, can, we, see, my, like, go, he, she, you, are).

    ConceptIDs: 141-160

    ASMT - Practice & Assessment¶

    Concepts related to practicing skills, achieving mastery, and monitoring student progress.

    ConceptIDs: 161-175

    APPL - Application Skills¶

    Higher-level skills where students apply their knowledge to create and manipulate words.

    ConceptIDs: 176-185

    CAPS - Capstone Skills¶

    Advanced skills representing the culmination of kindergarten reading instruction, including independent reading.

    ConceptIDs: 186-195
