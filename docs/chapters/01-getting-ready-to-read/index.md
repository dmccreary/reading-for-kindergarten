# Chapter 1: Getting Ready to Read

## Overview

This foundational chapter introduces children to the world of reading by building essential pre-reading skills. Children will develop an understanding of spoken language, learn to listen carefully, and discover that printed words carry meaning.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:

- **Remember**: Identify that spoken words are made up of sounds
- **Understand**: Explain that print carries meaning
- **Apply**: Demonstrate proper book handling (left to right, top to bottom)

## Chapter Summary

Getting ready to read starts with understanding that the words we speak can be written down and read. This chapter helps young learners make the connection between spoken and written language while building the listening and attention skills needed for reading success.

## Key Vocabulary

- **Print**: Written or typed words
- **Letter**: A symbol that represents a sound
- **Alphabet**: All the letters in order (A-Z)
- **Word**: A group of letters that means something

## Activities

1. **Story Time**: Listen to a story and point to words as they are read
2. **Book Walk**: Explore a book's cover, pages, and pictures
3. **Letter Hunt**: Find letters in the classroom environment
4. **ABC Song**: Sing the alphabet song together

## Prerequisites
